About Nashville Golf

A City That Punches Above Its Weight

Nashville has quietly emerged as one of the premier golf destinations in the American South. The city's diverse landscape — from the rolling hills of Percy Warner Park to the scenic banks of the Cumberland River — provides a stunning natural backdrop for some of the region's most celebrated courses. Whether you're a local or visiting between shows, Music City has a course for your game and your budget.

Why Golf in Nashville?

  • Year-round golf weather — mild Tennessee winters mean you can play almost any time of year
  • World-class course design — courses designed by PGA champions and legendary architects
  • Stunning scenery — rolling hills, river views, and lush hardwood forests make every round memorable
  • Something for every budget — from $14 munis to $72 championship tracks
  • A thriving golf community — active tournaments, clinics, and events throughout the year

Planning Your Round

Nashville enjoys a mild four-season climate that makes year-round golf possible. Spring and fall offer the most comfortable playing conditions, with mild temperatures and lush green fairways. Summers can be warm and humid, so early morning tee times are recommended. Winters are generally mild enough to play, though occasional frost delays are possible.
