Ted Rhodes Golf Course

Ted Rhodes Golf Course is one of Nashville's most historic public golf facilities, and one of its most meaningful. The course is named for Ted Rhodes, a pioneering African-American golfer who fought to break the color barrier in professional golf during the 1940s and 50s. Originally laid out in 1953 as the Cumberland Golf Course with just nine holes, the facility was redesigned and expanded to 18 holes in 1992 by Gary Roger Baird, ASGCA.

Situated along the north Nashville banks of the Cumberland River, Ted Rhodes offers links-style golf in an urban setting that is genuinely enjoyable for players of all skill levels. Large greens provide generous targets for approach shots, and the course's walkable layout makes it a popular choice for golfers who enjoy carrying their bag. Wildlife is a bonus — the lakes and river support turtles, fish, muskrats, and the occasional family of beaver.

Highlights

  • Named for trailblazing golfer Ted Rhodes, who helped break golf's color barrier
  • 18 links-style holes along the Cumberland River
  • Large greens — one of the most forgiving target areas in Nashville
  • Easy to walk — one of the most walkable courses in the city
  • Rich wildlife habitat along the river corridor
  • Home of the Duffers Golf Association, which raises funds for Sickle Cell research
  • Outstanding value — one of Nashville's most affordable rounds

Course Details

Ted Rhodes plays to 6,669 yards from the blue tees at a par 72, with a course rating of 71.8 and slope of 120. A fourth set of tees at 5,010 yards makes the course accessible for beginners and junior golfers. The layout opens with a 555-yard par 5 and features two long par 5s total — the back nine opens on a 534-yard hole as well. The course's relatively modest slope rating reflects its forgiving nature, though the river and lakes that border several holes add genuine risk for wayward shots.

Green Fees

As a Nashville Metro municipal course, Ted Rhodes offers exceptional value — among the lowest green fees of any 18-hole course in the city. Walk-on rates typically run $13–$17 for 9 holes and $26 for 18 holes. Cart rental is $8 per nine holes per player. Senior rates apply Monday through Thursday for golfers 60 and over. Tee times can be booked up to seven days in advance by phone or in person.

The Signature Hole

Holes 11 through 17 are widely considered the most scenic stretch on the course, hugging the Cumberland River and offering views of the water on nearly every shot. Hole 8 provides a sweeping look back toward North Nashville that rewards golfers willing to pause mid-round and take in the surroundings.

Amenities

Ted Rhodes features a pro shop and clubhouse with basic food and beverage service. The course is home to the Duffers Golf Association, a longstanding group of golfers who host an annual tournament to benefit Sickle Cell Anemia research and support Meharry Hospital. Cart rental and club rental are available on site.

Getting There

Located at 1901 Ed Temple Blvd, Nashville, TN 37208, just north of downtown near the Tennessee State University campus. Call (615) 862-8463 for tee times. Tee times are recommended but walk-in play is welcome based on availability.
