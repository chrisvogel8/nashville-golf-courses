Twelve Stones Crossing Golf Club

Twelve Stones Crossing Golf Club sits in Goodlettsville just 15 minutes north of downtown Nashville, and it is one of the more demanding public rounds you can play in Middle Tennessee. The course plays over 6,800 yards from the back tees with a course rating of 73.3 and a slope of 145 — numbers that put it firmly in championship territory and well above the difficulty profile of most daily-fee courses in the area. The routing rides dramatic elevation changes across rolling terrain, with Madison Creek threading through the property as a recurring hazard and scenic centerpiece. Even lies are hard to find, blind shots come with some regularity, and the TifEagle Bermudagrass greens are fast. For players who want a genuine test, Twelve Stones delivers it.

The course first opened as a private golfing community, but an economic downturn took a significant toll on conditions and ultimately on the course's playability. After reopening as a public golf course in 2014, new ownership and management have spent the years since working to restore the property to its original quality — and the current state of the facility reflects those efforts. The greens have been reseeded with TifEagle Bermudagrass, the fairways are Bermuda tee to green, and the property now includes a Learning Center with Trackman technology, a club repair shop, an independently operated grill, a driving range, and a full lesson program. Memberships, when available, fill up — the club hit fully subscribed status as of March 2026.

Course Details

Twelve Stones earns its slope rating of 145. The course plays across terrain that doesn't offer many flat lies — the elevation changes are genuine, not gentle, and they affect shot-making on nearly every hole. Madison Creek crosses or borders multiple fairways, demanding both distance control off the tee and precise iron play into greens that are often elevated, angled, or tucked behind natural features. The TifEagle Bermudagrass greens are fast and true, which rewards good putting but exposes any weakness in speed control. Bunkers are placed throughout at positions that force decision-making rather than just penal recovery.

The course is a par 72 designed by Bill Bergin and plays to just over 6,800 yards from the tips, with multiple tee options that make it more approachable for mid-to-high handicap players while preserving the full challenge for scratch golfers and competitive play. The nines were rerouted after reopening, and the current layout is the one golfers will find on the scorecard. Conditions have been steadily improving since the 2014 reopening under current management, and regulars note consistent week-to-week progress in surface quality.

The Learning Center

The Learning Center is the newest addition to Twelve Stones' practice facilities and one of the most serious instruction environments at any public course in the Nashville area. It houses Trackman launch monitor technology, enabling shot data-driven instruction, club fitting, and virtual range sessions that can replicate famous courses from around the world. The facility operates year-round regardless of weather, making it a resource for winter practice and off-season improvement that most Nashville-area golfers don't have access to at their home course. Lessons are taught by Jacob Nolen and Chris Wantz, who work from each golfer's existing swing rather than imposing wholesale changes — an approach suited to players at all levels from beginners to competitive amateurs. Contact the course at (615) 239-8945 to schedule a fitting or lesson.

The Repair Shop

Twelve Stones operates an on-site club repair facility — useful for regripping, reshafting, or loft and lie adjustments without needing to ship clubs off-property. Contact the pro shop for current services and turnaround times.

Green Fees

All daily rates include a cart. Weekday rates (Monday–Thursday) are $60 for 18 holes for regular players and $49 for seniors age 65 and over. Weekend rates (Friday–Sunday) are $72 for regular players and $59 for seniors. Nine-hole rounds are only available after 4:00 PM — $45 on weekdays and $48 on weekends. Spectators and riders during the week are $25; an extra cart for a weekday rider or spectator runs $30 if available. No weekend spectators without advance approval. Rates are subject to fluctuation based on demand — book online early for the best available rate. Tee times are bookable through the club's website at twelvestonesgolfclub.com.

Memberships

As of March 1, 2026, Twelve Stones Crossing Golf Club memberships are fully subscribed. The club is not accepting new members at this time but is maintaining a waiting list. Contact golf@twelvestonesgolfclub.com to be added to the waitlist.

Getting There

Twelve Stones Crossing Golf Club is located at 1201 Twelve Stones Crossing, Goodlettsville, TN 37072, approximately 15 minutes north of downtown Nashville. Call (615) 239-8945 or visit twelvestonesgolfclub.com to book tee times. The club also has an app available on iOS and Android.
