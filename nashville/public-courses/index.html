GreyStone Golf Club

Public Course — Designed by 10-Time PGA Tour Winner Mark McCumber

GreyStone Golf Club in Dickson is one of the most decorated public courses in the Nashville area — a Mark McCumber design that has hosted PGA Tour Qualifying School, the Tennessee State Open multiple times, and the 2025 Tennessee State Junior Amateur. At 7,046 yards from the tips with dramatic elevation changes and McCumber's signature mounding, GreyStone is a genuine test that rewards commitment and punishes indecision.

Old Fort Golf Club

Public Course — Golf Inc. 3rd Best Public Renovation in the USA (2024)

Old Fort Golf Club in Murfreesboro was transformed by a landmark 2024 renovation that earned national recognition — Golf Inc. ranked it the 3rd best public course renovation in the country, and Golf Digest gave it both an Honorable Mention and 3rd-place nod for best newly renovated affordable course. The 7,214-yard par 72 along the Stones River, adjacent to Civil War-era Fortress Rosecrans, is now playing as well as any public course in Middle Tennessee.

Montgomery Bell State Park Golf Course

Public Course — Golf Digest Top 100 Public Courses in America

Set inside Montgomery Bell State Park 40 minutes west of Nashville, this Certified Audubon Cooperative Sanctuary course has earned a place on Golf Digest's Top 100 Public Courses in the USA. The par 71 layout plays 6,311 yards with Champion Bermuda greens and 419 Bermuda fairways across pristine state park terrain. The on-site Lodge on Lake Acorn makes it one of the few true stay-and-play destinations in the Nashville area.

Indian Hills Golf Club

Public Course — Stones River Setting, Penn-Links Bentgrass Greens

Indian Hills Golf Club in Murfreesboro is one of the most complete public golf experiences in Middle Tennessee. The Stones River meanders through multiple holes on this par 72, 6,800-yard layout, while Penn-Links bentgrass greens provide one of the finest putting surfaces at any public course in the region. An indoor Golf Academy with video capture technology and a 10,000 sq ft clubhouse put Indian Hills in a league above most daily-fee operations.

Nashboro Golf Club

Public Course — Heroes' Tuesday: Military, Police & Fire Play Free

Nashboro Golf Club is one of Nashville's most respected daily-fee courses, playing 6,887 yards at a par 72 with a slope of 132 — a genuine championship test just 5 miles south of BNA airport. Nashboro's Heroes' Tuesday program offers complimentary green fees every Tuesday for active and retired military, police, and firefighters. Indoor simulators, an active Men's Golf Association, and Mini Verde greens complete the package.

Pine Creek Golf Course

Public Course — Best Greens in the Nashville Area

Pine Creek Golf Course in Mount Juliet has built a reputation as the best-value round in the Nashville area — a claim backed by its consistently high marks for bent grass green quality, a log cabin clubhouse with patio dining, and a layout that offers two distinct nines: open, links-style golf on the front and tighter, tree-lined traditional play on the back. At par 72, 6,676 yards with a slope of 124, it's a legitimate test at a price that competes with any course in its tier.

Twelve Stones Crossing Golf Club

Public Course — Slope 145 — One of Nashville's Toughest Public Tracks

Twelve Stones Crossing Golf Club in Goodlettsville plays over 6,800 yards with a slope of 145 — placing it among the most demanding rated public courses in the Nashville area. Madison Creek winds through the property, dramatic elevation changes create uneven lies on nearly every hole, and TifEagle Bermudagrass greens run fast. A Trackman-equipped Learning Center, on-site club repair shop, and memberships that are currently fully subscribed signal a course trending in the right direction.

Harpeth Valley Golf Center

Public Course — Nashville's Premier Golf Learning Facility

Harpeth Valley Golf Center on Old Harding Road is the city's go-to destination for golfers who take improvement seriously. The facility centers on a 9-hole par-3 River Course along the Harpeth River, but the real draw is the infrastructure around it: a massive LED-lit driving range with natural grass and mats, a short game area with four target greens and bunkers, six PGA/LPGA instructors, and senior and junior discounts all day. A complete golf education environment at municipal prices.

McCabe Golf Course

Public Course — Nashville's Oldest Public Course — 27 Holes

McCabe Golf Course is Nashville's oldest public course, with original holes dating to 1942 and a two-level, 27-station driving range that opened in 2012 as the best practice facility in Nashville public golf. Three nine-hole layouts — Original, North, and South — offer a variety of 18-hole combinations, all on Bermuda fairways with rebuilt green complexes. From April 15 through November 15, range balls are hit off natural Bermuda grass.

VinnyLinks Golf Course

Public Course — Named for Vince Gill — Home of First Tee Tennessee

VinnyLinks Golf Course in East Nashville's Shelby Park has hosted golf since 1930 — and U.S. Open champion Lou Graham learned the game here as a boy. Named for country music legend Vince Gill, whose support funded its 2001 Donald Ross-inspired renovation, VinnyLinks is the Nashville headquarters of First Tee of Tennessee. The par-28 nine-hole walking course, irons-only driving range, and 9,000 sq ft practice green offer some of the best short-game practice in the city for $14–$16.

Ted Rhodes Golf Course

Public Course — Named for a Pioneering Trailblazer in American Golf

Ted Rhodes Golf Course honors one of golf's great barrier-breakers — an African-American Nashville native who fought to integrate the PGA Tour in the 1940s and 50s. The 18-hole links-style layout along the Cumberland River plays 6,669 yards at par 72, with large greens, a walkable layout, and one of the most accessible slopes (120) at any full-length course in the city. Holes 11–17 hug the river and offer some of the most atmospheric public golf in Nashville.

Cedar Crest Golf Club

Public Course — Cedar-Lined Fairways on 175 Acres in Murfreesboro

Cedar Crest Golf Club in Murfreesboro delivers a distinctly Tennessee experience — 175 acres of rolling countryside, cedar tree-lined fairways, classic split-rail fencing, and a bentgrass/Bermuda layout that plays 6,828 yards at par 72. Designed by John Floyd in 1999, the front nine is friendly enough for beginners, but the back challenges players with its #1 handicap hole — a 445-yard par 4 — before finishing on a 580-yard par 5.

Franklin Bridge Golf Club

Public Course — Championship Golf Along the Harpeth River in Franklin

Franklin Bridge Golf Club — formerly the Crossing Golf Course — plays 6,968 yards along the Harpeth River in Franklin. Multiple holes run parallel to the water, offering one of the most scenic stretches of golf in Williamson County. The Crow's Nest restaurant and competitive green fees with one of the best junior rates in the area ($9 weekday / $18 weekend for under-17) make Franklin Bridge a strong value in a county where golf can get expensive quickly.

Smyrna Golf Course

Public Course — Town of Smyrna — 27 Holes, Outstanding Practice Facilities

Smyrna Golf Course is one of the most complete municipal facilities within reach of Nashville — 27 holes (18-hole regulation plus a 9-hole First Tee executive course), an 8,000 sq ft putting green, four-tee driving range with practice bunker, and competitive pricing with a slope of 120. The 18-hole course plays 6,453 yards at par 72 and is open year-round, 20 minutes southeast of downtown.

Percy Warner Golf Course

Public Course — Nashvillians Have Learned the Game Here Since 1937

Percy Warner Golf Course has been part of Nashville's golf fabric since 1937, tucked inside one of the largest urban parks in the country on the city's west side. A recently completed restoration — funded through a public-private partnership with Metro Parks, the Tennessee Golf Foundation, and Friends of Warner Parks — delivered new greens, rebuilt surrounds, a new irrigation system, and restored tee boxes. At $17 weekday, it's the most scenic walk in Nashville public golf.

Nashville National Golf Links

Public Course — Limestone Bluffs and Sycamore Creek, Northwest of Nashville

Nashville National Golf Links is a hidden gem northwest of Nashville near Pleasant View — an 18-hole par 72 built around dramatic limestone bluffs and Sycamore Creek, with elevation changes and natural terrain that feel unlike anything you'll find at the city's flatter riverside courses. Green fees include a complimentary range bucket and cart, and a First Tee program runs through the facility. The staff's love of the game shows throughout.

Long Hollow Golf Course

Public Course — City of Gallatin — Friendly, Affordable, 25 Minutes from Nashville

Long Hollow Golf Course is the kind of well-managed, no-fuss municipal course that every city should have. Owned by the City of Gallatin and operating since 1982, the par 70 layout plays 6,047 yards with a slope of 115 — approachable enough for new golfers, satisfying enough for regulars. With a driving range, putting green, and cart sheds on site, Long Hollow has everything you need for a relaxed afternoon of golf at honest prices.

Shelby Golf Course

Public Course — Home of Tennessee's Longest-Running Junior Golf Tournament

Shelby Golf Course in East Nashville holds a special place in the city's golf history as the longtime home of the School Days Junior Golf Tournament — the longest-running golf tournament in Tennessee. A 2024 upgrade brought TifEagle Bermuda greens to the 18-hole municipal layout, matching the premium putting surface now found at Harpeth Hills and other top Metro courses. Affordable Metro pricing, cart rental at $8/nine, and a park setting make Shelby a community fixture.

Cheekwood Golf Club

Public Course — 9-Hole Executive Course Along the Harpeth River, Franklin

Cheekwood Golf Club near Franklin is a 9-hole executive course with a mix of four par 3s and five par 4s that plays in about 90 minutes — an efficient and genuinely useful round for beginners, juniors, and experienced players who want concentrated short-game and iron work. The Harpeth River frames several holes. For anyone in Williamson County looking to fit golf into a busy schedule without a full-day commitment, Cheekwood is the right call.

Riverside Golf Links

Public Course — 27 Holes + Executive Course Along the Cumberland River

Riverside Golf Links in Old Hickory is one of the largest public golf operations in the Nashville area by total playable holes — a 27-hole main facility with a 9-hole executive course, all set along the Cumberland River. The par 70 layout has been under new ownership since 2020, with improvements to surfaces and an active junior program through Operation 36 on site. Weekday 18-hole green fees start at $26.63 walking — among the most affordable full-rounds near Nashville.

Oak Hills Golf Course

Public Course — Oak-Lined Fairways in Greenbrier, 25 Minutes North of Nashville

Oak Hills Golf Course is a semi-private 18-hole layout in Greenbrier (Robertson County) that has been part of the local golf community since 1968. A 2015–2016 renovation rebuilt the greens, rerouted the front nine back to the original 1968 design, and elevated the course considerably. The two-story clubhouse features The Loft Bar with a full bar, grill, and outdoor deck overlooking the course — a social atmosphere that goes well beyond what most courses in this price range offer.

Centerville Municipal Golf Course

Public Course — Small-Town Municipal Golf Since ~1940, 45 Minutes Southwest

Centerville Municipal Golf Course is a genuine off-the-beaten-path find — a nine-hole Town of Centerville muni operating since around 1940, with recently renovated greens, a par 36 layout at 3,060 yards, and a full restaurant in the clubhouse. At approximately $20 for nine holes with a cart, it's one of the cheapest rounds within 50 miles of Nashville. The unhurried pace and Hickman County countryside setting are half the appeal.

Dogwood Hills Municipal Golf Course

Public Course — City of Portland — 9-Hole Muni, 35 Miles North of Nashville

Dogwood Hills Municipal Golf Course is the City of Portland's public golf facility — a nine-hole layout designed by Jerry Lemons that plays 3,125 yards with a slope of 121, 35 miles north of Nashville. Walking green fees start at $20 for nine holes and $30 for 18, with solid senior and junior discounts. Annual memberships run $850–$2,000. For North Nashville-area golfers looking for a low-key round close to home, Dogwood Hills delivers.
