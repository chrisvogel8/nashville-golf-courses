Oak Hills Golf Course

Oak Hills Golf Course has been the home course for Robertson County golfers since 1968, when the Greenbrier Recreation Club opened its original nine holes on rolling farmland north of Nashville. The course expanded to 18 holes in 2001 and underwent a meaningful renovation in 2015 and 2016 that included new green construction, green rebuilds, and a rerouting of the front nine back to its original 1968 layout. Today Oak Hills operates as a semi-private club with open public play — 25 minutes north of downtown Nashville, with a layout that rewards local knowledge and a price point that makes frequent play genuinely affordable.

Designed by Scott Wilkerson and Harold Hooper, the course plays to a par 70 at 5,685 yards from the back tees, with sweeping terrain, water features on multiple holes, and the oak-lined fairways that give the course its name. General Manager Jeff Hastings, PGA, oversees the operation. The club runs weekly games, hosts an active tournament schedule each season, and offers membership options for golfers who want more than just a tee time. For many players in Greenbrier, Goodlettsville, Ridgetop, and Springfield, Oak Hills is simply where they go to play golf.

Course Details

The Oak Hills layout plays through the naturally rolling terrain of Robertson County, with elevation changes that add visual interest and demand thoughtful club selection throughout the round. Water features come into play on multiple holes, and the oak-lined fairways that defined the original 1968 routing remain a defining characteristic of the course. The 2015–2016 renovation improved the quality of the putting surfaces and restored the front nine to Wilkerson and Hooper's original design intent, which regulars regard as the stronger routing of the two nines. At par 70 and 5,685 yards, Oak Hills rewards accuracy and course management over raw distance — a layout where local players have a genuine advantage and first-time visitors quickly learn to respect the terrain.

The Clubhouse and The Loft Bar

Oak Hills has invested meaningfully in its clubhouse experience. The ground floor features a renovated pro shop stocked with gear, apparel, and accessories, along with the Fireside Room — a sunny, large meeting space available for private events and group rentals. Upstairs, The Loft Bar is a full bar and grill with an outside deck overlooking the course, making it one of the more enjoyable 19th-hole experiences at a public course in the Nashville region. The upper event space can be rented separately for private gatherings. The combination of two distinct indoor spaces and an outdoor deck gives Oak Hills flexibility for outings and events that many similarly-priced courses can't match.

Green Fees

Oak Hills offers some of the best value in the northern Nashville suburbs. Weekday (Monday through Thursday) rates run approximately $20–$35 for 18 holes depending on time of day and cart selection. Weekend rates (Friday through Sunday) run approximately $25–$42. Junior rates are available for players aged 15 and under; note that juniors 15 and younger are not permitted to rent golf carts. Membership options are available with benefits including discounted rates on food, beverage, and merchandise, invitations to member-only events, preferred event room rental pricing, and fishing access on the course. Call (615) 643-4505 for current rates and tee time availability, or book online through the club's website.

Weekly Games and Events

Oak Hills runs a regular schedule of weekly games throughout the season — a core part of what keeps the club's membership and regular player base engaged. The annual tournament calendar typically opens in early spring and runs through the fall. The club's online shop sells gift cards and merchandise, and an email club keeps regular players informed of upcoming events, specials, and course news. Group outings can be coordinated through the club's events staff, with catering available through The Loft Bar for larger gatherings.

Getting There

Oak Hills Golf Course is located at 1928 Hygeia Road, Greenbrier, TN 37073, approximately 25 minutes north of downtown Nashville in Robertson County. Open Monday through Friday 8:00 AM – 4:30 PM; Saturday and Sunday 7:00 AM – 4:30 PM. Call (615) 643-4505 for tee times, membership inquiries, and event bookings.
