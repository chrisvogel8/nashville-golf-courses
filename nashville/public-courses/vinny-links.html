VinnyLinks Golf Course

VinnyLinks Golf Course sits inside Shelby Park on the banks of the Cumberland River in East Nashville, five minutes from downtown. The course is one of the most historically layered pieces of golf ground in Nashville — the land has hosted golf since 1930, when it opened as Riverview Golf Course, and a U.S. Open champion learned the game here as a boy. Today it carries the name of country music legend Vince Gill, whose inspiration and financial support made possible the $1.3 million renovation that transformed a closed, deteriorated facility into the First Tee of Tennessee's flagship Nashville site. It reopened on May 9, 2001, and has been introducing kids to golf and its values ever since.

VinnyLinks is a nine-hole par-28 walking course playing 1,210 yards — one par 4 and eight par 3s — with Bermuda greens, three tee sets, an irons-only driving range, a 9,000 square foot practice green with greenside bunkers, and a full-service pro shop offering lessons, club fitting, and club repair. It is open March through November. Green fees for adults start at $14. It is the most accessible, affordable round of golf in Nashville proper, and it is genuinely excellent short-game practice for players of any level.

The Story Behind the Name

The ground that VinnyLinks occupies has a longer golf history than almost any other public site in Nashville. The original Riverview Golf Course opened here in 1930, was renovated in 1967, and received a new clubhouse in 1972. Among the young golfers who came up playing Riverview was Lou Graham, who went on to win the 1975 U.S. Open at Medinah — one of the most distinguished careers to come out of Nashville golf.

Riverview closed after the 1998 season. When the Tennessee Golf Foundation and Metro Parks set out to rebuild the facility as a First Tee site, they needed a champion. They found one in Vince Gill. The country music star brought both financial support and personal conviction to the project, and when the rebuilt facility opened on May 9, 2001, it was named the VinnyLinks in his honor. The historical marker on the property reads: "Through his inspiration and financial support thousands of children from the Middle Tennessee area have been introduced to the sport of golf." The course's primary purpose, then and now, is to provide every child — regardless of race, gender, or economic background — the opportunity to learn golf and the life values it teaches.

Course Details

VinnyLinks plays as a par 28 over nine holes and 1,210 yards. The layout consists of one par 4 — the opening hole at 285 yards — and eight par 3s. Three tee sets give golfers of different abilities the option to play from appropriate distances, making the course genuinely usable for complete beginners and still worthwhile for experienced players who want concentrated iron and wedge work. The Donald Ross-inspired redesign gives the layout more character than a standard executive course — the greens have genuine contours and the routing makes thoughtful use of the Cumberland River setting. The course is walking only; no riding carts operate here.

The irons-only driving range is a specific and practical feature. For golfers who want to work on the 150-yards-and-in part of their game without the noise and distraction of driver practice, VinnyLinks offers something most Nashville practice facilities do not. The 9,000 square foot practice green with greenside bunkers rounds out a short-game practice environment that is hard to match at any price point in the city.

First Tee of Tennessee

VinnyLinks is the Nashville headquarters of First Tee of Tennessee, one of the country's leading junior golf development programs. First Tee uses golf as a platform to teach life skills — integrity, respect, perseverance, confidence — to young people aged 5 through 18. Programming at VinnyLinks runs throughout the season and includes structured classes at multiple skill levels from introductory through advanced competitive tracks. Juniors are given priority access on weekdays during summer and after school in the fall. Adults are welcome to play the course, but understanding that the facility exists primarily to serve young players shapes what VinnyLinks is and why it matters. For program schedules and enrollment, contact Program Director Sydney Copeland at (615) 645-9863 ext. 103 or scopeland@tngolf.org, or visit firstteetennessee.org.

Green Fees

VinnyLinks offers the most affordable public golf in Nashville. Adult green fees are $14 for a weekday round and $16 on weekends. Senior rates are $9 and junior rates are $7 regardless of day. Pull cart rental is $5. Club rentals are $5 for junior sets and $6 for adult sets. A bucket of range balls is $7. The course is walking only — no riding carts.

Hours and Season

VinnyLinks is open March through November and closed December through February. During the current period of standard time, hours are Monday–Friday 7:30 AM–6:00 PM, Saturday 7:00 AM–6:00 PM, and Sunday 8:00 AM–6:00 PM. Hours extend to 7:00 PM after the spring time change. The driving range currently closes at 4:00 PM, shifting to 5:00 PM after the time change. Hours and days are subject to change — confirm before visiting, particularly early and late in the season.
