Montgomery Bell State Park Golf Course

Forty minutes west of Nashville, tucked inside 3,850 acres of Dickson County hardwood forest, Montgomery Bell State Park Golf Course has quietly earned a reputation as one of the best-value rounds in the entire region. Golf Digest has recognized it as one of the Top 100 Public Courses in the United States — a distinction that surprises first-time visitors until they step onto a course that plays through dense tree corridors, rolls over genuine elevation changes, and putts out on Champion Bermuda greens that regulars describe as fast and true. For the price of a mid-tier daily-fee course, Montgomery Bell plays like something considerably better.

The course was originally designed by George Cobb in 1973, redesigned by Gary Roger Baird in 1988, and updated again by Jerry Lemons in 2016. It is a Certified Audubon Cooperative Sanctuary, and the wildlife that defines the park — deer, wild turkey, Canada geese — shares the course freely with golfers. The par-71 layout plays to 6,311 yards from the back tees with a course rating of 69.3 and slope of 121, with three additional tee options ranging down to 4,981 yards. Each spring, the course hosts the Dogwood Classic, traditionally the opening event of the Tennessee tournament season.

Course Details

Montgomery Bell is a par-71 layout built into the rolling, heavily wooded terrain of the state park. Almost every par 4 and par 5 on the course is a dogleg — bending in both directions — with elevation changes that demand thoughtful club selection off the tee. The fairways are generous enough to reward accuracy without punishing the slightly wayward drive, but the tree-lined corridors keep the ball in play on every hole. Longer hitters will find that a driver is often not the right call, as the fairways can run out on downhill holes. The par 3s play tough, but the shorter par 5s offer legitimate birdie opportunities for golfers who keep it in the short stuff. The fast Champion Bermuda greens are consistently the most praised feature of the course and hold up well through the season.

Green Fees

Montgomery Bell offers some of the best pricing on the Tennessee Golf Trail. In-season rates (April 1 – November 17) run $55 with cart on weekdays and $64 on weekends and holidays for 18 holes. Walking rates are $35 on weekdays and $44 on weekends. Off-season rates (November 18 – March 31) drop to $48 with cart or $30 walking for 18 holes any day of the week. Seniors aged 62 and over receive reduced rates — $45 with cart on in-season weekdays, $25 walking. Juniors aged 17 and under walk for $24 any day. An afternoon special applies in-season after 2:00 PM: 18 holes with cart for $45 on any day of the week. All rates are subject to change and do not include tax.

Annual passes are available for frequent players. The standard 7-day pass (greens fees only, no cart) is $1,700 per year. A full all-inclusive pass covering cart and range balls runs $2,100. A Tennessee Golf Trail annual pass covering all Bear Trace and state park courses is available for $2,800. Senior versions of each pass are offered at reduced rates.

The Stay-and-Play Experience

Montgomery Bell is one of the few public courses in the Nashville area with genuine on-site lodging. The fully renovated 117-room Lodge sits on the banks of Lake Acorn and features private balconies, a full-service restaurant and bar, an outdoor pool, and a fitness facility. For golfers looking to make a weekend of it, the combination of a Golf Digest-recognized course, comfortable lodge rooms, and state park amenities — hiking, fishing, kayaking — makes Montgomery Bell a standout option in the region. Cabins and campsites are also available for a different kind of stay. Contact the park directly for lodging reservations and stay-and-play packages.

Tee Times and Events

Tee times are required seven days a week and can be reserved up to six days in advance online or by calling the clubhouse. The course staff handles group outings and tournament events, including catering through the clubhouse, which seats up to 100 guests and features a spacious outdoor veranda. Contact the pro shop for the tournament outing packet and current event availability.

Getting There

Montgomery Bell State Park Golf Course is located at 1020 Jackson Hill Road, Burns, TN 37029, approximately 40 minutes west of Nashville in Dickson County. Call the golf course clubhouse at (615) 797-1850 for tee times, rates, and event inquiries.
