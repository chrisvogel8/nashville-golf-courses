Centerville Municipal Golf Course

Centerville Municipal Golf Course is the kind of course Nashville-area golfers drive past on the way to somewhere else — and then kick themselves for not stopping sooner. Owned and operated by the Town of Centerville in Hickman County, this nine-hole municipal layout has been welcoming golfers since around 1940, making it one of the oldest continuously operating public golf facilities in Middle Tennessee.

At roughly 45 minutes southwest of Nashville, Centerville is a genuine off-the-beaten-path find. The course sits on Skyview Drive overlooking the quiet countryside of Hickman County, and the pace of play, the friendly staff, and the price point all reflect the unhurried character of small-town Tennessee golf. Recent improvements to the greens have elevated the experience considerably — the small, sloped putting surfaces are now well-conditioned and significantly more receptive than they were in previous years, rewarding accurate iron play while still offering recoverable misses.

Highlights

  • Town of Centerville-owned — genuine small-town municipal golf
  • One of the oldest public courses in Middle Tennessee — operating since approximately 1940
  • 9 holes with two sets of tees, playable as an 18-hole round
  • Bermuda fairways with broad landing areas — forgiving off the tee
  • Small, sloped greens recently renovated — fast and challenging
  • Restaurant inside the clubhouse — full food service, not just a snack bar
  • Renovated restrooms and clubhouse facilities
  • Clubhouse available for private events with catering services
  • Annual passes available for frequent players

Course Details

Centerville Municipal plays to 3,060 yards from the back tees at a par 36 across nine holes, with a course rating of 35.0 and slope of 113. Two sets of tee boxes allow golfers to play a full 18-hole round with variety — the alternate tees shift the character of several holes meaningfully enough to make the back-nine feel distinct from the front. The Bermuda fairways are generous in width, creating a welcoming environment for beginners and high-handicappers, but the small, undulating greens put a premium on approach accuracy. Missing these greens in the wrong spot can turn a straightforward hole into a scramble.

Green Fees

Centerville Municipal offers some of the most affordable golf within 50 miles of Nashville. Green fees run approximately $20 for nine holes with a cart — among the lowest rates of any course in the area. Annual membership passes are available for those who make the drive regularly and offer significant savings over daily rates. Walk-up play is welcome. Call (931) 729-2474 for current pricing and availability.

The Clubhouse

The clubhouse at Centerville Municipal is a genuine surprise. Unlike many small-town municipal courses where the "clubhouse" is a converted garage with a coffee maker, Centerville's facility has a full restaurant serving hot food — a detail that makes the drive from Nashville worthwhile in its own right. The renovated restrooms and event space round out a clubhouse that punches well above the course's green fee price point. The facilities are available for private events with full catering services, making it a popular choice for local fundraising tournaments and company outings.

Who It's For

Centerville Municipal is the perfect course for golfers who want to escape the crowded Nashville-area tee sheets and play a relaxed, affordable round without fighting for a tee time. The layout is ideal for beginners getting comfortable with the game, families introducing kids to golf for the first time, and seasoned players looking for a low-pressure afternoon away from the city. The drive through Hickman County on the way out is half the appeal — this is Middle Tennessee at its most genuinely unhurried.

Getting There

Located at 2351 Skyview Dr, Centerville, TN 37033, approximately 45 minutes southwest of downtown Nashville via US-70. Call (931) 729-2474 for hours and current rates. Walk-up play is welcome.
