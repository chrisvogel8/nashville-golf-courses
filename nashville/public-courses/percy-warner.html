Percy Warner Golf Course

Percy Warner Golf Course has been part of Nashville's golf fabric since 1937, making it one of the oldest public golf facilities in the city. Nestled inside the rolling hills of Percy Warner Park — one of the largest urban parks in the United States — the nine-hole layout winds through hardwood forest just minutes from Belle Meade and Green Hills, surrounded by the same natural landscape that draws hikers, trail runners, and wildlife watchers to the park year-round. For nearly nine decades it has served as the place where Nashvillians learn the game, and that character remains at the core of what Percy Warner is today.

The course recently completed a major restoration carried out through a public-private partnership between Metro Parks and Recreation, the Tennessee Golf Foundation, and Friends of Warner Parks. The project delivered new greens, rebuilt green surrounds, a new 50-year irrigation system, improved drainage and stormwater control, and restored tee boxes throughout. The renovation transformed a facility that had been under-invested for years into a properly maintained course worthy of its setting — one of the most beautiful pieces of land any Nashville golfer will play on.

Course Details

Percy Warner's nine-hole layout plays to 2,600 yards at a par 34, with a course rating of 32.2 and slope of 108. It is a short course by any measure, but the tree-lined fairways and small, undulating greens make it more demanding than the yardage suggests. The terrain rolls naturally with the park landscape, and each hole feels genuinely different from the last — a quality that comes from a routing designed to work with the land rather than flatten it. The restored greens have drawn praise from players who remember the course before the renovation, and the improved surrounds allow for more creative short-game play than the previous conditions permitted. A second set of tee markers allows the course to play as a full 18 holes for those who want a longer round.

The Restoration

Percy Warner's restoration addressed decades of deferred capital investment in one of Nashville's most historically significant golf facilities. The 50-year-old irrigation system was replaced with modern infrastructure, improving turf quality, eliminating leaks, improving drainage and stormwater control, and freeing up maintenance labor hours that had been spent managing an outdated system. New greens were built and existing greens were rebuilt to improve both playing quality and consistency. Tee boxes were restored and new tees were added to improve turf quality and enhance playability for golfers of all ages and abilities. The Friends of Warner Parks endowment established through the partnership now supports ongoing equipment, maintenance, junior golf programming, and staffing needs for the course — a structural improvement that goes beyond the physical renovation itself.

Green Fees

Percy Warner operates on Nashville Metro municipal pricing, updated as of April 1, 2025. Standard green fees are $17 for 9 holes on weekdays and $22 on weekends. Playing 18 holes (two loops) runs $34 on weekdays and $44 on weekends. Seniors aged 60 and over receive discounted rates Monday through Thursday: $12 for 9 holes and $24 for 18 holes. Riding cart fees are $8 per 9 holes per player — note that Percy Warner is primarily a walking course, and riding carts are available on a limited basis for players with proof of a disability. Pull cart rental is $2.50.

Annual Memberships

Annual memberships are available for those who play Percy Warner regularly. Resident memberships run $1,000 for a regular membership and $600 for seniors. Non-resident rates are $1,200 and $720 respectively. Metro employees receive the same rate as seniors. A $5 member surcharge applies per nine holes at all Metro courses. Youth memberships are no longer being sold. All membership inquiries can be handled at the course.

Hours of Operation

Percy Warner's hours vary by season. During peak season (May 1 through Labor Day), the course opens at 7:00 AM on weekdays and 6:00 AM on weekends, closing at 6:00 PM. Fall and winter hours shift to later opening times with closing at dark. Tee times can be reserved up to seven days in advance by calling the course directly or stopping by in person.

Getting There

Percy Warner Golf Course is located at 1221 Forrest Park Drive, Nashville, TN 37205, inside Percy Warner Park on the west side of Nashville near Belle Meade. Call (615) 862-8492 or email nashvillegolf@nashville.gov to reach Course Manager Kevin Forte.
