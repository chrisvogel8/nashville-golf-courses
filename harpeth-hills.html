Harpeth Hills Golf Course

Harpeth Hills Golf Course is widely regarded as one of the finest public golf values in the Nashville area. Set within the stunning natural landscape of Percy Warner Park, the course offers a peaceful and scenic round of golf that feels worlds away from the busy city just minutes away.

Operated by Metro Nashville Parks, Harpeth Hills has built a loyal following among Nashville golfers who appreciate its challenging layout, mature hardwood setting, and unbeatable combination of quality and affordability.

Highlights

  • 18-hole public course in Percy Warner Park
  • Surrounded by mature hardwood forest
  • One of the best value rounds in Nashville
  • Challenging layout for experienced golfers
  • Peaceful, natural setting away from the city

Course Details

Harpeth Hills plays to a par 72 and stretches approximately 6,600 yards from the back tees. The course winds through rolling, wooded terrain with significant elevation changes that make club selection and course management essential. Narrow fairways bordered by dense tree lines demand accuracy, and the well-conditioned greens reward precise approach play.

The Signature Hole

The par-4 12th hole is a fan favorite and one of the most visually striking holes on the course. Playing downhill through a narrow chute of tall hardwoods, the tee shot requires precision to find the fairway below. The approach then plays back uphill to an elevated green with steep falloffs on both sides, making it a hole that demands respect from start to finish.

Amenities

Harpeth Hills features a comfortable clubhouse with a snack bar and golf shop stocked with essentials. The practice facility includes a driving range and putting green. The surrounding Percy Warner Park offers additional outdoor recreational opportunities, making it a great destination for a full day outdoors.

Getting There

Harpeth Hills Golf Course is located at 2424 Old Hickory Boulevard in Nashville, within Percy Warner Park on the city's southwest side. Tee times can be booked through Metro Nashville Parks online or by phone, and the course is open to the public year-round.
